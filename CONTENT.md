# Content Guide

## Voice

Warm. Quiet. Personal. Not preachy, not pushy.

**Like:**
- "My father made this for a stranger. He does that."
- "Someone in Oregon is holding this right now."
- "You don't have to believe anything. Just know you're loved."

**Not like:**
- "Transform your faith journey with our handcrafted crosses"
- "Official Every Day Cross merchandise available now"
- Religious jargon or guilt

## Post Types

### 1. The Making

**What:** Photos/videos of hands working wood, tools, process.

**Caption style:**
```
90 years old. Still making. Still giving.

This one is for [name/story if known].

#EveryDayCross
```

### 2. The Request

**What:** Someone's story, posted to the community.

**Format:**
```
REQUEST: For my daughter, starting chemo next week. She needs to know she's not alone.

Location: Michigan, USA

[Optional: photo of recipient, or just text]
```

### 3. The Fulfillment

**What:** Maker shows the cross they made, mentions the request they're answering.

**Format:**
```
Fulfilling a request for [brief description].

Cross is [wood type/material]. Note says [snippet if you want to share].

Heading to [location] tomorrow.

#EveryDayCross
```

### 4. The Exchange

**What:** Both sides post—requester receiving, maker giving. The moment of connection.

**Caption style:**
```
It arrived.

A stranger made this. Read my story. Took time. Sent love.

I don't know what to say except: thank you. I'll carry it.

#EveryDayCross
```

### 5. The EDC

**What:** Anyone posts their Every Day Cross—what they carry, why.

**Caption style:**
```
My Every Day Cross:

[Object description]. [Who gave it or where it came from]. [What it means].

What's yours?

#EveryDayCross
```

## Storytelling Rules

1. **Names matter.** Use first names when possible. "This is for Sarah" beats "this is for someone."
2. **Specifics over abstractions.** "Oak from my backyard" beats "quality materials."
3. **The maker is part of the story.** Show hands, mention who made it.
4. **The recipient is the point.** Their story, their need, their moment.
5. **God is present, not loud.** Faith is assumed, not advertised.

## Response Guidelines

When someone comments:
- **Thank them** — genuinely, specifically
- **Ask a follow-up** — "Who would you request one for?"
- **Invite them in** — "DM us if you want to make one"

Never:
- Ask for money
- Push religious conversion
- Ignore genuine stories

## Crisis Posts

Sometimes requests will be heavy—suicide, terminal illness, deep grief.

**Do:**
- Respond with care
- Share mental health resources in your country
- Let the community rally

**Don't:**
- Promise miracles
- Ignore the weight of it
- Make it about you
