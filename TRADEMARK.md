# Trademark & Usage Guidelines

## Every Day Cross™

The name "Every Day Cross" and its associated logo are protected. This document explains what that means for you.

## The Short Version

We want this idea to spread. We don't want it exploited.

You can participate freely. You cannot commercialize the name without permission.

## What You Can Do (No Permission Needed)

- **Make and give away crosses** — this is the whole point
- **Request crosses** for yourself or people you love
- **Share your story** using #EveryDayCross
- **Start local groups** using the concept (church groups, maker circles, etc.)
- **Use the logo** on personal posts, non-commercial websites, hand-made crosses you give away
- **Write about us, photograph us, film us** — journalism, art, personal projects

## What Requires Permission

- Selling products (t-shirts, jewelry, accessories) with "Every Day Cross" name or logo
- Using the name/logo in fundraising campaigns
- Creating "official" merchandise or authorized products
- Claiming endorsement or partnership without agreement

## Why We Do This

Our father is 90. He never charged for a cross. He never will.

If someone starts selling "Every Day Cross™ Faith Apparel" at a mall kiosk, or running a GoFundMe "officially affiliated with Every Day Cross," that twists what this is.

Protection isn't about control. It's about keeping the door open for the right reasons.
