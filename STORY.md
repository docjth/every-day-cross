# The Story

> *Small wooden crosses. Big love.*

## The Beginning

My father turns 90 this year. For years—decades, maybe—he's been making wooden crosses by hand. Small ones, palm-sized, smoothed by his hands.

He gives them away.

To strangers. To friends. To people he meets once and never sees again. He hands them a cross and says something like, *"Someone made this for you. Someone cares."* Sometimes he says more. Sometimes he says nothing at all.

He never charges. Never asks for anything back. Just makes, gives, moves on.

## Opening the Door

My sister and I watched this for years. The crosses piling up on his workbench. The stories he came home with—a cashier who cried, a neighbor who kept it in her pocket for months.

We asked: what if this could be bigger? Not bigger as in more famous. Bigger as in more hands, more stories, more people carrying love they didn't expect.

**Every Day Cross** is that.

## How It Works

1. **Someone needs a cross** — they share their story (who it's for, why)
2. **Someone else connects** — a maker reads it, feels it, decides to craft and send a cross
3. **Both share the moment** — photos, words, the exchange

My father's crosses started this. Now it's anyone's hands, anyone's story.

## The Name

Every Day Cross.

A play on EDC—Every Day Carry, the things people keep in their pockets, on their keys, close to them daily.

But this is something different to carry. Not utility. Love. A reminder, on hard days, that someone made something for you. That you're seen.

## Every Day Carry vs. Every Day Cross

| Every Day Carry | Every Day Cross |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Protection** — Knife, flashlight, multi-tool for physical threats | **Protection** — A reminder that you're loved, seen, not alone |
| **Readiness** — Prepared for what might go wrong | **Readiness** — Grounded for whatever comes |
| **Communication** — Phone, radio, whistle to call for help | **Communication** — A wordless message: *someone cares* |
| **Utility** — Tools that do things | **Meaning** — An object that *means* something |
| **Self-reliance** — I can handle this | **Connection** — I'm not handling this alone |
| **Bought** — Curated, purchased, upgraded | **Given** — Made by hand, passed freely, no strings |

Same pocket. Different weight.

## The Protection

We trademarked the name. Not to own it—to protect it.

So no one sells "Every Day Cross™ Official Merchandise" at a mall. So no one fundraises "officially affiliated" without us knowing. The idea stays true: free crosses, given away, hand to hand.

If you want to use the name for something aligned—shirts for a church retreat, a documentary, whatever—just ask. We usually say yes. We just want to know.

## Who Runs This

My sister and I. Our father still makes crosses. We just opened the door wider.
